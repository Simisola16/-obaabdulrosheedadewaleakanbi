About The Oluwo

His Royal Majesty, Oba Abdulrosheed Adewale Akanbi

Oluwo of Iwo Land is the paramount ruler of Iwo Kingdom, a first class natural ruler in Yorubaland. His Royal Majesty, Oba Abdulrosheed Adewale Akanbi, ascended the throne on November 19, 2015 as the 16th Oluwo of Iwo Land.

Oba Akanbi is known for his progressive leadership, commitment to the development of Iwo Land, and his efforts to promote Yoruba culture and traditions globally. He is a visionary leader dedicated to fostering unity and progress in his domain.

Under his reign, Iwo Land has witnessed significant development in infrastructure, education, and cultural renaissance. The Oluwo is also recognized for his advocacy for peace and his initiatives to empower the youth and women in his kingdom.

Born into a royal lineage with deep roots in Yoruba heritage, Oba Akanbi brings a wealth of experience from his background in education and community service. Before ascending to the throne, he served in various leadership roles that prepared him for the responsibilities of rulership.

The Oluwo's leadership style emphasizes inclusivity, innovation, and sustainability. He has implemented numerous programs aimed at improving healthcare, education, and economic opportunities for all residents of Iwo Land.

Internationally, Oba Akanbi represents Yoruba culture at various global forums, strengthening cultural ties and promoting Nigeria's rich heritage. His diplomatic efforts have earned him recognition as a bridge-builder between traditional institutions and modern governance.

Legacy of Leadership

The Oluwo Tradition

The Oluwo of Iwo has been a cornerstone of Yoruba culture for centuries, serving as both spiritual and temporal leader of the Iwo people. The institution represents continuity, wisdom, and the preservation of ancestral values.

Throughout history, Oluwos have played pivotal roles in maintaining peace, fostering development, and upholding the rich cultural heritage of the Iwo Kingdom. Each Oluwo brings their unique vision while honoring the traditions of their predecessors.

Oba Abdulrosheed Adewale Akanbi continues this noble tradition, blending ancient wisdom with modern approaches to governance. His reign marks a new chapter in the storied history of the Oluwo institution.
